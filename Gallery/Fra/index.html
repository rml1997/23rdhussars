Hospital

The Regiment came across the Chateau de Cuy which the Germans had converted into a military hospital. Over 200 enemy staff and wounded soldiers were taken prisoner.
23H Story, Page 115 and Page 116.

  • [Photo 1] 1944, hospital entrance.
  • [Photo 2] 1944, two 23H men oversee the searching of German prisoners.
  • [Photo 3] the Chateau, pre-war.
  • [Photo 4] the Chateau, modern view.
In addition, two 23H men, Sergeant Harris and Trooper White, who had both been wounded and captured the previous night, were freed.
The Regiment arrived here on 20 Aug.

On Jul 17 the Regiment crossed via two newly-constructed Bailey Bridges, codenamed LONDON. These were situated about 6/700 metres south of Pegasus Bridge (codenamed EUSTON) and were the first Bailey Bridges to be built during the campaign in Europe. London Bridge 1 was completed 2 days after D Day by 17 Field Company Royal Engineers.

'Crossroads' photo

The following photo appears in several sources covering the events of the time. Taken on 31 Jul 1944, it shows a Sherman tank (T147851) standing guard at a crossroads in St Martin as vehicles of the 8th Bn The Rifle Brigade head towards the newly-taken bridge over the Souleuvre River. The building immediately behind the tank is the Hotel des Voyageurs, photos of which are shown below. The photo appears in the 23H Story Page 117. Inexplicably, however, the scene is described as "the Falaise - Argentan road", which is clearly wrong.
