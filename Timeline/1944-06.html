June 1944

Aldershot

DateWar DiaryNotes
1-8Preparations to move. 
41 x 15-cwt truck received for LAD. 1 x 15-cwt truck sent away.  
5Regimental small arms competition at Ash Ranges. 
75 x Stuart III received. 1 x Stuart V, 9 x Shermans V and 4 x Humber Scout Cars sent away.  
8Major AD Wigan and 55 Other Ranks depart with light vehicles. 4 x Shermans V sent away.  
9 Advance party leaves under control of Major Dunkerly. 1 x 15-cwt truck received. 1 x Humber Scout car and 1 x 15-cwt Bedford Truck sent away.  
11Main body leaves Aldershot for Marshalling Area. Light party embarked Tilbury.  
13Light party disembarked M Beach Coursuelles-sur-Mer.  
14-15Main party embarked at Gosport. Geoff Bishop's narrative starts on 14 Jun. He landed in France on 23 Jun as part of 270 FDS.
15-16Main party disembarked at M Beach. 23H Story Page 35.

France

16-25Regiment concentrated at Coulombs.  
 

Operation EPSOM

26 F Echelon advanced behind 15th (S) Division. First contact with enemy by "C" Squadron at 19.00 hours. Lieut Da Cunha wounded by shrapnel at St Manvieu. 1 x Other Rank wounded. 23H Story Page 41+.

DIED THIS DAY
27 07.00 hours. "A" Squadron engaged 88 m.m. guns south of Cheux. "B" and "C" Squadrons advance in support of 15th (S) Division towards Mondrainville.

12.00 hours. "B" and "C" Squadrons up to the line of the main road Caen - Villers. Held up by antis.

19.00 hours. "C" Squadron followed by "B" and half of HQ make bridgehead over River Odon at Tourmauville and harboured there. Remainder of HQ and "A" Squadron held up at Mondrainville.

Lieuts. Kerton & Weiner missing believed killed. Captain Taylor and Lieuts. Russell, Byard and Riley wounded. 16 x Other Ranks killed. 20 wounded. 12 Missing. 		Bishop Page 13.

Of those listed as "missing": Lieuts. Kerton and Weiner and 3 x Other Ranks had been taken prisoner. Weiner was later to escape and rejoin the Regiment.

DIED THIS DAY
28 05.30 hours. Advance towards Point 112. "B" Squadron leading.

12.00 hours. "B" and "C" Squadrons established north half of Point 112. HQ with "A" Squadron in reserve move up. 8 x Shermans from FDS come up and put under command of "B" Squadron.

15.30 hours. Relieved on position by 3rd R.T.R. owing to shortage of ammunition. Withdrew to orchard south side of Tourmauville.

Captain R.G. Clark and Lieut Helyar killed. 2 x Other Ranks killed. 5 x Other Ranks wounded. 6 x Other Ranks missing. Lieut D.F. King suffering from exhaustion.
2 x 3-ton lorries received. 		Geoff Bishop commanded one of the "8 x Shermans from FDS". Bishop Page 14+.

DIED THIS DAY
29 13.30 hours. From orchard move forward to position south of the Cavalry at Tourmauville. Enemy counter-attack developed at 15.00 hours from the west (Area of Esquay). Beaten off by 19.00 hours.

3 x Other Ranks missing and 3 x Other Ranks wounded.		 3 men had been taken prisoner.

DIED THIS DAY
30 Withdraw to Cully.
3 x Other Ranks killed and 3 x Other Ranks wounded.		 DIED THIS DAY
